# Lore

## My approach to lore

Before getting into the document proper I also want to present my approach to lore as a concept related to creating Hunvre.

While I personally appreciate lore, history, geography and other parts of worldbuilding, Hunvre is not a worlbuilding project, it is first and foremost a video game (it's just a mod but I'm referring to the intended mode of consumption of the experience). What that entails is that I mainly care about delivering an enjoyable video game experience and that lore should be there to "enable" that, it also means that I don't consider lore to be sacred at all, if retcons need to happen because they allow an interesting mechanic to be implemented they will happen.

## Physical context

The mod takes place on a generational space ship named Midas with a rotating wheel structure travelling through space.

The topside of the wheel hosts a minor part of the population, it is not too dissimilar to how most artists depict that kind of structure as, you can think of Elysium or Mass Effect's Citadel. There's artificial sunlight, plants, grass, roads and houses.

The underside of the wheel hosts the majority of the people living on the ship, it is where the productive infrastructure is located. It is made of dense city like structures connected to each other through a tram like system. It is the main place people will be exploring in the mod.

## Chronological context

The events of the mod take place after enough time for the people living there to have any experience of earth beyond archived depictions of it.

## The cataclysm

The Midas being limited in resources needs to periodically travel through or near sources of materials. The catalyst for the events of the mod is the decision to go through a deceptively innocuous cloud of gas to harvest.

The "gas" is in fact the 3 dimensional part of a 4 dimensional structure or entity that as soon as it made contact with the Midas started infiltrating it. For the sake of convenience it'll be called the Dreamer here but it doesn't have to be what people in the setting call the phenomenon.

The Dreamer seeks to interact with human brains, and from the moment the ship started going through it the people aboard started having vivid hallucinations among other symptoms, culminating in a condition where people going to sleep under the influence of it enter a catatonic state before slowly "disappearing" (they in fact shift into the fourth dimension).

Almost immediately after the first disappearances euzhvils started appearing within the Midas.

## Euzhvils

Euzhvils are hostiles creatures with limited sentience, they are expressions of human dreams, fears and emotions.

They are made of skin, flesh and bones and should be reminiscent of human beings in some way, be it featuring human limbs, facial features or even mannerisms for example.

They should not feature clothing or other accessories although those can be represented as part of their body if needed, a hammer could be represented as a misshapen hand for example.

Most of them should be only partially present in 3 dimensions at all times, slipping in and out of it as they move around.

That also means they can have some fantastical physical features, they could appear to be floating above the ground or have limbs that don't look connected to their bodies.

They aren't hostile to each other. They can either be actively looking for the remaining survivors or simply shambling around.

Euzhvils can be imbued with some amount of "magic" powers, since euzhvils are connected to the brains of any other euzhvil or survivor they can for example make someone feel like they're drowning or suffocating, make them think they're blind or things of that nature.

## Science-fiction

The mod is science-ficiton but only as much as it allows fiction to enable either interesting mechanics or esthetics.

What that means is you can safely assume that technology that exists in the real worls will exist in Hunvre in the same capacity. If exceptions exist and are important enough they will be listed as sub segments here.
